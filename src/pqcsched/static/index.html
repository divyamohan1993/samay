Why this is urgent

The migration is mandatory, massive, and on a clock.

Post-quantum migration isn't optional or far-off. Three things make it brutal:

🕰️

Harvest now, decrypt later

Attackers are recording your encrypted traffic today to decrypt it the moment a quantum computer arrives. Anything that must stay secret for years is already at risk.

⚖️

Mandated, with deadlines

NIST, the NSA (CNSA 2.0), and national programs require the move, with hard dates (2030–2035). Miss them and it's a compliance and audit failure, not a maybe.

🧩

Thousands of tangled pieces

A real estate has thousands of crypto usages that depend on each other (a service can't go quantum-safe before its certificate authority does), limited capacity each quarter, and assets that can't move until vendors ship support.

What SAMAY does

It turns a panicky guess into a provably optimal roadmap.

✕ How everyone plans it today

  • “Migrate the highest-risk thing first.” A gut-feel ranking.
  • Ignores the order dependencies force on you.
  • Can’t see a quarter ahead — so it misses regulatory deadlines.
  • No way to prove the plan is any good.

✓ How SAMAY plans it

  • Treats it as the scheduling problem it actually is.
  • Computes the provably optimal order — which asset, which quarter.
  • Respects every dependency, budget limit, and mandated deadline.
  • Proves no schedule leaves less data at risk.

You feed it your cryptographic inventory (a standard CBOM) or a what-if scenario; it returns the migration roadmap and shows you, in hard numbers, how much safer the optimal order is than the one you'd have guessed.
